1. Define the Problem¶

Movies are a form of escape from reality and have long been art that is appreciated beyond language. Movies are not only for entertainment purposes, they also make us think and react to societal issues. Movies bring stories to life and some even act as inspiration. However, not all movies are appreciated for their content and value. Documentaries are not the most popular genre among audiences despite being shot realistically while Romance and Action-comedies are much appreciated and viewed. This might be due to a wrong review or a personal opinion. Hence, it is important to analyze and predict the quality of the Movie through reviews carefully. I will use the IMDB dataset to classify movies into two categories. The model will be built and improved upon gradually to produce the most precise results.

5. Developing a model that does better than a baseline¶

The Naive Bayes Classifier is used as the baseline model for comparison and is trained using the full review training dataset. The test dataset is then used to evaluate the model. Evaluation Metrics such as Accuracy, Precision, and Recall scores are calculated and the model seems to have a 69% accuracy in classifying the movie reviews accurately. An underfit model is first developed using minimum requirements and is fairly simple. Relu is used as the last layer activation function. The RMSprop optimizer is used for this project to train the models since it has a higher convergence speed and can reduce the training time of the models working with large datasets such as our case in this project(What is rmsprop 2022). Since RMSprop has fewer parameters compared to other optimizers it is also easier to tune its hyperparameters.

The Loss and Accuracy values are calculated for each of the models and are visually represented using graphs. The accuracy and loss values are analyzed to ensure that the model is performing as efficiently as possible. Accuracy is used as an evaluation metric during compilation of all the models. However, since the dataset is split into training, testing, and validation, the number of values available in each set is no longer the same, affecting the accuracy measure as accuracy requires all datasets to have the same amount of values. Hence, to ensure the model's highest performance, both the accuracy and loss values are plotted for the underfit, overfit, and regularized models.

7. Regularising and Tuning hyperparameters¶

Overfitting can be overcome in many ways but the two most popular methods are used in this project. The first method is adding a drop out and the second method is L1 and L2 Regularisation. Dropout is considered to be the most popular and effective method of regularization. Dropout causes a random number of output features to drop during model training. This randomness reduces the overfitting of the model. Next, weight regularization to the model. The weight regularization restricts model complexity by causing the weights of the models to only take minimum values. This causes the weights to become more regular. A small cost is added to the loss function of the model in the form of L1 and L2 regularisation. The two models are then combined to form a third model which is the final model of this project. The final model of this project is then trained using the full training and validation set and the accuracy and loss are calculated.

Conclusion¶

In Conclusion, I have managed to develop a model using the Hold-out evaluation protocol with two different types of regularization methods used which are Dropout and L1 and L2 regularization to reduce the overfitting of the model. The two models with different regularization methods are combined into a single third model.
